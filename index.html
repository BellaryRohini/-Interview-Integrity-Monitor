    What this tool can and can't tell you
    • It looks for signs that are common when someone is using a hidden AI assistant — things like a connected audio-routing device, answers that all take an unusually similar amount of time to start, and speech that sounds a little too polished. It can't identify which specific AI tool, if any, was used.
    • It won't catch someone using a second device that's completely out of view — for example, a phone off-camera or another person feeding them answers off-mic.
    • Every candidate is compared against their own first answer, not a fixed rule, so naturally well-spoken candidates aren't flagged just for being articulate. That said, this is based on only a handful of questions, so treat the result as a helpful signal, not a final answer — a human should always make the actual call.
